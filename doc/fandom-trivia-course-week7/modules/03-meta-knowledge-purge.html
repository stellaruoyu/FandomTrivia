Meta-Knowledge Purge

Every question about actors, directors, and behind-the-scenes trivia was systematically removed. Here's why — and how.

What Is Meta-Knowledge?

Meta-knowledge is information about a piece of fiction, not from inside it. If you're doing a Star Wars quiz, a meta-knowledge question would be "Who played Chewbacca?" The in-universe version would be "What is the name of the Wookiee homeworld?"

This week, every trivia file was audited and every meta-knowledge question was rewritten. The rule is now a hard constraint, enforced by the project's AI rules file:

📜
The New Rule

"For all quizzes in this project, there should be 0 questions about out-of-universe meta-knowledge, such as who acted in a role, where a movie/show was filmed, who directed it, etc. Quizzes must focus strictly on in-universe lore and story details."

Scope of the Purge

The meta-knowledge cleanup touched quizzes across the entire site. Here are the universes that got reworked:

🚀

Star Wars

Questions about actors' accents, camera directions, and production details were replaced with character and planet lore.

🌙

Twilight

Meta questions about casting and film locations swapped for vampire biology and werewolf pack history.

⛰️

Percy Jackson

Behind-the-scenes trivia replaced with Greek mythology and demigod abilities.

🦸

Harry Potter

Actor names and film trivia swapped for spell lore and wizarding world history.

🎯
Why This Matters

A fan of Star Wars knows Kashyyyk. A casual movie-goer might know Peter Mayhew played Chewbacca. The new questions reward fandom, not IMDb browsing. This is a deliberate product decision that makes the site more appealing to dedicated fans.

Locking It In: AGENTS.md

The meta-knowledge rule was written into .agents/AGENTS.md — the project's custom rules file for AI assistants. This is a newly created file this week:

CODE 

# FandomTrivia Custom Rules

- **Quiz Content Constraints**: For all quizzes
  in this project, there should be 0 questions
  about out-of-universe meta-knowledge, such
  as who acted in a role, where a movie/show
  was filmed, who directed it, etc.
  Quizzes must focus strictly on in-universe
  lore and story details.
PLAIN ENGLISH

AGENTS.md is like a permanent memo to every AI assistant that works on this project.

It says: no questions about actors, directors, or production trivia.

Every quiz must test what you know about the fictional world itself — the story, characters, and lore.

This ensures consistency whether a human or an AI writes the next quiz.

🔒
Engineering Best Practice

This is a textbook example of "automating policy." Instead of manually reviewing every future quiz, the team baked the rule into the AI's instructions. Now every AI-generated question is automatically constrained to in-universe lore — no reviews needed.
