Their presence is filtered through the social lens of Jessica Stanley, who provides the expositional data required to categorize them as "uncanny" outsiders. The differentiators are strictly physical: chalky pallor, purplish shadows under the eyes, and a thermal contrast where their skin remains "ice-cold." The Biology lab sequence serves as the first proof-of-concept for a supernatural "glitch" in the environment. Edward’s rigid, hostile reaction—precipitated by the scent of Bella’s strawberry shampoo—introduces a high-stakes tension that moves the narrative from social observation to critical interaction.
