Death’s End: A Comprehensive Fandom Trivia Repository

1. Strategic Context and Narrative Architecture

Death’s End, the concluding volume of Cixin Liu’s Remembrance of Earth’s Past trilogy, represents the apex of speculative world-building, offering an unparalleled data set for high-stakes trivia. The narrative’s strategic value is derived from its staggering chronological span—extending from the Fall of Constantinople in 1453 to the heat death of the universe—and its relentless integration of complex technical frameworks such as the pulse-propulsion logistics of the Staircase Program and the game-theoretic foundations of Dark Forest Deterrence. This repository is curated to challenge the "deep-lore" enthusiast by navigating the profound sociological shifts of the Deterrence Era and the rigorous physics of high-dimensional fragments.

The primary differentiator of this dataset is its radical genre-pivot: the transition from the "Age of Magic" in a localized 4D fragment to the hard science fiction of the Crisis and Deterrence Eras. This architecture creates a unique difficulty curve, requiring users to synthesize historical anecdotes with technical parameters. The following sections serve as a curatorial commentary on these pivotal eras, culminating in a verifiable JSON repository designed for high-precision application.

2. Category I: The Death of the Magician and Crisis Genesis (1453 – Crisis Era Year 4)

The "Age of Magic" provides the narrative’s first architectural proof that the laws of physics are not constant, but are subject to cosmic geometry. The 1453 Constantinople fragment establishes that "magic" was merely the human interpretation of high-dimensional openness. This era serves as a direct thematic precursor to Yang Dong’s existential crisis; her discovery that "Nature is not natural" mirrors the magician Helena’s realization that the universe is a "coffin" once the 4D fragment departs.

Curatorial Trivia Focus The following questions target the specific evidence of 1453 and the geological implications of Yang Dong’s "Earth without life" simulation.

* Question 1: Within the secret chamber of Hagia Sophia, what specific evidence in the sealed sarcophagus proved Helena had utilized 4D "openness"? (Focus: The count of seven fresh grapes).
* Question 2: In the "Earth without life" simulation, what was the ultimate geological fate of the planet’s oceans? (Focus: Evaporation/Venus-like greenhouse effect leading to a "dry" husk).
* Question 3: What specific architectural anomaly in the Blachernae quarter served as proof of high-dimensional spatial folding? (Focus: The intermittently missing top of the minaret).

The "So What?" Layer: Analysis of Significance The detail of the seven grapes is not a mere plot point; it is a technical demonstration of 4D geometry, where a "sealed" 3D container remains open from a higher dimension. Similarly, the simulation results underscore the profound concept that life is a geological force. Without life to regulate the atmosphere, Earth’s evolution shifts from a habitable world to a barren, Venus-like desert, proving that the universe’s physical state is manufactured by biological presence. The end of this localized 4D "magic" leaves humanity in a cold 3D reality, necessitating the brute-force maneuvers of the Staircase Program.

3. Category II: The Staircase Program and the PIA (Crisis Era Years 1–7)

The Staircase Program, overseen by Thomas Wade, exemplifies the "advance at all costs" mandate of the early Crisis Era. In a period where human progress was capped by sophon interference, the program was a desperate strategic gambit to achieve quasi-relativistic speeds by repurposing the world’s nuclear arsenal. This era is defined by extreme pragmatism, where the preservation of a single human brain outweighed the survival of a whole body.

Curatorial Trivia Focus These questions assess command over the technical specifics of the program and the gift that initiated its final phase.

* Question 4: To accelerate the probe to one percent of lightspeed, what was the exact count of nuclear bombs distributed along the acceleration leg? (Focus: 1,004 bombs).
* Question 5: What is the alphanumeric designation of the star purchased by Yun Tianming as a gift for Cheng Xin? (Focus: DX3906).
* Question 6: Wade’s psychological testing of Cheng Xin involved a famously ruthless hypothetical question regarding the sale of a family member.
* Question 7 (High Difficulty): What is the primary technical differentiator between "Hibernation" and "Cryopreservation" as defined by the text? (Focus: Cardiopulmonary bypass systems vs. flash-freezing without life support).

The "So What?" Layer: Differentiating Preservation The distinction between Hibernation (minus-fifty-degrees Celsius with a cardiopulmonary bypass) and Cryopreservation (minus-two-hundred-degrees Celsius "flash-freeze") is the pivot upon which the mission’s success rested. The mass-constraints of the Staircase Program (reduced to 180kg for the payload) made a life-support-heavy hibernation system impossible, forcing the strategic decision to send only a brain. This technical hurdle serves as the tragic catalyst for Yun Tianming’s trajectory into the heart of the Trisolaran fleet.

4. Category III: The Deterrence Era and the Swordholder (Deterrence Era Years 12–61)

The Deterrence Era introduced the "Swordholder," a sociological entity tasked with the psychological burden of mutually assured annihilation. This era explores the fragile equilibrium where human society flourishes under Trisolaran knowledge while simultaneously drifting toward a "feminized" state that undermines the very deterrence keeping it alive. The trial of the Bronze Age crew serves as the narrative’s moral anchor for the dark realities of deep-space survival.

Curatorial Trivia Focus This section targets the game-theoretic logic of deterrence and the legal consequences for those who survived the Doomsday Battle.

* Question 8: Upon their return to Earth, what specific charges were brought against the crew of the Bronze Age? (Focus: Murder in the first degree and crimes against humanity).
* Question 9 (Complex MCQ): According to Deterrence Game Theory, what is the critical threshold for the "degree of deterrence" required for success? (Focus: Conceptual distractors regarding individual vs. committee broadcast probability).
* Question 10 (High Difficulty): True or False: "Nyctohylophobia" led to the mandatory and widely supported shutdown of all high-power antennas on Earth.

The "So What?" Layer: Deterrence Game Theory The "degree of deterrence" is the most vital parameter of the era. While a committee’s probability of broadcasting is near zero due to the "feminization" of society’s values, an individual like Luo Ji maintains a probability between 91.9% and 98.4%. This conceptual gap explains why the Swordholder must be a singular, often "savage" entity from the Common Era. The eventual mandatory shutdown of antennas due to Nyctohylophobia (the pathological fear of the "dark forest") represents humanity’s transition from cosmic optimism to a state of absolute, shivering isolation.
